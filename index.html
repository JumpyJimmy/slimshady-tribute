Eminem Tribute

Eminem a.k.a Marshall Mathers a.k.a

Slim Shady

The best selling hip-hop artist in history

A Timeline of Eminem's Rap Career:

  • 1972 - Born in St. Joseph, Missouri
  • 1996 - Eminem and five other rappers formed The Dirty Dozen (D12). Eminem also released his debut album 'infinite' which was a commercial failure.
  • 1999 - Eminem released 'The Slim Shady LP' in February and would end up being certified triple platinum by the end of that year.
  • 2000 - 'The Marshall Mathers LP' was released and it sold 1.76 million copies in the first week breaking US records for fastest-sellng hip hop album and for fastest-selling solo album. The album was certified Diamond in 2011.
  • 2002 - 'The Eminem Show' was released and it was another success, selling 1.33 million copies in the first week and was the best-selling album of 2002.
  • 2004 - 'Encore' was a success but did not do quite as well as his previous albums as sales were driven partially by the first single 'Just Lose It'.
  • 2007 - Eminem was hospitalised after a methadone overdose. After being checked out and some unfortunate events, his drug use "ramped back up to where it was before". After seeking help from a rehabilitation counselor, he has been sober since April 2008.
  • 2009 - Comeback. Eminem released 'Relapse', although it did not sell as much as his previous albums, it was a success and re-established his presence in the hip hop world.
  • 2010 - 'Recovery' was released selling over 741,000 copies in the first week and topping the Billboard 200 chart. 'Recovery' was the best selling album of 2010 making Eminem the first artist in history to have two year-end best-selling albums. 'Recovery' is the best selling digital album in history.
  • 2013 - 'The Marshall Mathers LP 2' was released making Eminem the first American artist with seven consecutive UK number-one albums also securing his position as Canada's best selling artist and 2013's best selling album.
  • 2017 - Eminem released 'Revival' topping the US Billboard 200 chart again. However, it had mixed reviews from critics and was considered his worst album.
  • 2018 - 'Kamikaze', his first surprise album was released in response to the backlash from his previous album. It topped the US Billboard 200 again making it his ninth album in a row to do so.
  • 2020 - Eminem released another surprise album 'Music to Be Murdered By' debuting number one at the Billboard 200 causing him to be the first artist to have ten consecutive debuting at number one in the US. A deluxe album 'Music to Be Murdered By - Side B' also was released without any prior notice and debuted at number 3 on the Billboard 200 later that year.
  • 2022 - Eminem was inducted into the Rock and Roll Hall of Fame.
